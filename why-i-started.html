Why I started
The Tech Mom Chronicles

On the AI revolution, staying curious after 17 years, and why learning never really stops — no matter how experienced you become.

DS
Divya Santhanam
Staff Technical Program Manager · ServiceNow
PublishedMarch 2026
Read time5 min read
CategoryPersonal

We are living in the middle of an AI revolution. You already know this — you feel it everywhere. In the tools you use at work, in the conversations happening in every industry, in the headlines every single morning. AI is not coming. It is already here, and it is moving fast.

As someone with 17 years in technology, I knew I had a choice: watch it happen, or lean into it. I chose to lean in. Not just to use AI as a tool in my day-to-day work as a Technical Program Manager — but to genuinely understand it. To learn how to build with it. To go from someone who thought building on the internet was complex and out of reach, to someone who actually does it.

"The website you are reading right now? I built it in 15 minutes — with the help of AI. And a few months ago, I would not have believed that was possible for me."

That moment — sitting back and realising what I had just created — is exactly why I started this space. Because if I felt that way, I know others do too. And I wanted to document the journey, out loud, so that others could see it was possible for them as well.

· · ·

What nobody tells you about being experienced

There is something quietly uncomfortable about being considered an expert. After 17 years, people come to you for answers. Your title reflects your track record. And somewhere along the way, there is a subtle pressure to already know things — to not appear uncertain, to not be the beginner in the room.

Building with AI felt like that for me at first. It seemed like a different kind of technical — one that belonged to developers and engineers and founders, not to program managers who spend their days in delivery governance and stakeholder alignment.

But I started anyway. I asked questions I felt I should already know the answers to. I made mistakes. I kept going. And what felt complex and out of reach when I began my career — I did it in 15 minutes, with the help of Claude.

The moment that started all of this

"I sat back, looked at a real live website I had just built on the internet, and thought — if I had known it was this accessible, I would have started years ago. That's exactly why I'm sharing this journey out loud."

This is a self-learning journey — and that's the point

I want to be completely honest about what this space is. It is not a thought leadership blog where I hand down wisdom from years of experience. It is a live, ongoing, self-learning journey — shared in real time, with all the uncertainty and excitement that comes with genuinely figuring something out.

As I grow in my career, I also want to show others — especially those who are experienced, established, and quietly wondering if it's too late to start something new — that you can still start small. You don't need to wait until you have it all figured out. You don't need to be a beginner by title to learn like one.

The message I want this space to carry:

Learning never stops. Not when you reach a senior title. Not when the industry considers you an expert. Not when you have 17 years behind you. The moment we stop being curious is the moment we stop growing — and I am not ready for that.

Because we learn every single day — we just don't always call it that

Think about the last week of your life. How many times did you figure something out for the first time? How many moments of quiet learning happened that you never even named as learning?

👩‍💻
At Work

A new tool, a different framework, a stakeholder dynamic you hadn't encountered before. Every project teaches you something the last one didn't prepare you for.

🍳
In the Kitchen

Trying a new recipe, improvising when you're missing an ingredient, discovering you are actually a better cook than you ever gave yourself credit for.

🎨
As a Parent

Helping with homework, supporting a school art project, explaining something you half-remember — and learning it all over again alongside your child, with fresh eyes.

Learning is not something that belongs only to students or people starting out. It is what keeps us curious, relevant, and — honestly — genuinely alive to the world around us. It just changes shape as we grow.

"Learning never stops. Not in tech, not in finance, not in the kitchen, not in motherhood. It just changes shape."

So — why this website?

Because I wanted to document the journey and share it with others who might need to see that it is possible. Because AI is not going away, and the best thing any of us can do is engage with it — learn it, use it, build with it — rather than wait for someone else to explain it to us.

And because, as a working mother with a demanding career, a financial learning journey just beginning, and the beautiful, exhausting, joyful chaos of family life — I wanted a space that holds all of it together. One honest, human space. Not polished. Not performed. Just real.

This is that space. I hope something here is useful to you. And if nothing else, I hope it makes you feel a little more confident that wherever you are in your journey — it is never too late to start something new.

Welcome to The Tech Mom Chronicles. I am so glad you found your way here.

— Divya
